# DBM-SI / ASI / FTI / PDS / CG Review and Outlook
### A Structural Intelligence Tech Stack, Grand Map, and Future Direction for Autonomous Systems

## DBM-SI Grand Map

> From Metric Structures and CCCs\
> to Policy Control, Function Tunnels, and Autonomous Structural Intelligence.

## Overview

This repository presents a unified review and outlook of the evolving:

- DBM-SI (Digital Brain Model – Structural Intelligence)
- ASI (Autonomous Structural Intelligence)
- FTI (Function Tunnel Intelligence)
- PDS (Policy Decision System)
- CGCCC (Calling Graph Common Concept Core)
- Elastic Trajectory Intelligence
- CCC-centered Structural Governance Systems

Rather than treating intelligence as purely statistical prediction or parameter scaling, this work investigates intelligence as:

- structural navigation,
- metric-space organization,
- CCC-preserved evolution,
- policy-controlled derivation,
- tunnel-preserved adaptation,
- and autonomous structural growth.

The project consolidates years of accumulated algorithms, runtime systems, architectural abstractions, and AI governance research into a single structural intelligence landscape.

## Core Thesis

The central thesis emerging from DBM-SI is:

> Intelligence is not merely prediction.\
> Intelligence is structural navigation inside feasible evolution tunnels under policy and stability constraints.

### 2. Policy Decision System (PDS)

PDS investigates policy-controlled structural runtime intelligence.

Core equation:

    PDS(u)=M(P(D(K(S(u)))))

where:

- S = Segmentation
- K = Knowledge
- D = Decision
- P = Policy
- M = Runtime Execution and Adaptation

![./docs/figures/Fig-003-PDS-CONTROL-PLANE.png](./docs/figures/Fig-003-PDS-CONTROL-PLANE.png)

Extensions include:

- CCC-Preserved Generation (CPG)
- Core-CCC-Preserved Analysis (CPA)
- Structural Decision Surfaces
- Policy-Separated Runtime AI

### 3. Elastic IR / FTI Frontier

Trajectory intelligence evolved into:

- Elastic Trajectory Intelligence
- Tunnel Navigation Intelligence (TNI)
- Adaptive Tunnel Navigation (ATN)
- Function Tunnel Intelligence (FTI)

FTI proposes that stable evolution occurs through:

- feasible structural tunnels,
- CCC-preserved derivation,
- elastic adaptation,
- and policy-stabilized trajectories.

This direction extends naturally into:

- biology,
- language,
- civilization evolution,
- collective learning,
- and autonomous AI systems.

## Relationship with LLMs and World Models

DBM-SI is not anti-LLM.

In fact, DBM-SI itself emerged partly through long-term human–LLM collaborative exploration.

DBM-SI proposes that future AI systems may require:

- a generative layer (LLMs),
- plus a structural control plane.

LLMs remain highly effective for:

- generation,
- semantic abstraction,
- compression,
- and reconstruction.

DBM-SI contributes:

- CC extraction,
- policy separation,
- structural routing,
- metric governance,
- trajectory stabilization,
- and autonomous structural auditing.
